1. Abstract

Global energy policy is increasingly converging on nuclear fusion as “the” next-generation solution.
While fusion is a promising high-density energy source, the global fixation on a single technological pathway introduces systemic fragility, geopolitical risk, and structural dependency.

This paper argues that humanity must transition not to “a new central power,” but to a distributed, redundant, self-repairing energy lattice, combining:
	•	printable thin-film solar photovoltaics (Canon-type industrial printing PV)
	•	vertical-axis wind turbines (VAWT)
	•	micro-hydraulic pressure generators in pipelines
	•	building-integrated solar geometries (octahedral / pyramidal PV surfaces)
	•	localized storage + microgrids

Such distributed systems reduce disaster vulnerability, decentralize control, scale rapidly, and align with natural-law principles such as circulation, redundancy, and adaptive stability.

Fusion is one option—not the only path.
A civilization choosing monocentrism will fail the moment its single pillar collapses.

	•	Centralized infrastructure = catastrophic failure domain
	•	Long downtime = economic paralysis
	•	Global inequality deepens due to ownership concentration
